Danjuma retires from May & Baker board
Feyisayo Popoola

May & Baker Nigeria Plc has announced the retirement of Lt. Gen. Theophilus Danjuma(retd) as a director and chairman of the board of directors.

The company said in a statement that Danjuma’s retirement, which took effect on September 27, 2019, followed his 16 years of service.

It said Danjuma was appointed to the board of the healthcare company in 2003, after which he led the company to many successes, prominent among which is the construction of one of the largest pharmaceutical manufacturing facilities in sub-Saharan Africa.

The statement read in part, “The facility is certified by the World Health Organisation for good manufacturing practice, one of the few African drug production companies to be so qualified.

“He also led the company in pioneering expansion into crucial areas of the healthcare industry including the perfection of a joint venture with the Federal Government to set up West Africa’s first private vaccine production company, Biovaccines Nigeria Limited.

“From a turnover of about N1bn, Danjuma grew the company to a turnover of about N10bn, with good annual profit and dividend records.”

The statement added that under Danjuma, the company won many national and international laurels including the National Productivity Order of Merit Award in 2018.

It said following his retirement from the board, Senator Daisy Danjuma, was appointed as the chairman of the board of directors of the company.
