Elumelu advises African govts on enabling business environment
Ife Ogunfuwa

The Founder of the Tony Elumelu Foundation and Chairman, United Bank for Africa, Tony Elumelu, has emphasised the important role of the government in creating an enabling environment for Small And Medium Enterprises to thrive.

Speaking at the Youth Konnekt Forum in Cape Verde on Friday, he advised entrepreneurs to embrace hard work, discipline and sacrifice on their entrepreneurial journey to success.

The African investor shared nuggets from his wealth of experience as an entrepreneur and success stories investing in Africa.

Speaking on the theme “African Youth: Foresight and Opportunities”, Elumelu charged the youths to imbibe discipline in all their dealings, a statement said on Friday.

He said, “Entrepreneurship is a long-term journey. There is no quick fix. You will make mistakes. You will have challenges. For you to succeed along this journey, you must be disciplined, make sacrifices and save today to achieve your goals.”

Participants at the forum were public sector leaders included the President of Cape Verde, Jorge Carlos de Almeida Fonseca OICVV; Prime Minister, José Ulisses de Pina Ceorreia e Silva, and development partners at the United Nations Development Programme.

Elumelu commended the government of Cape Verde for the strong, deliberate reforms to boost entrepreneurship in Lusophone Africa.

He stated that the future of the African continent was in the hands of the African youths and they must be empowered to achieve the ambition of a well-developed continent.
