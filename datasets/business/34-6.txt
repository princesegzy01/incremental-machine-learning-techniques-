Okomu Oil shareholders get N1.91bn interim dividend
Feyisayo Popoola

Okomu Oil Palm Plc has declared an interim dividend of N2 per 50 kobo ordinary share.

This amounts to N1.91bn to be received by the shareholders.

The company, in a notice filed at the Nigerian Stock Exchange on Tuesday, said the interim dividend was subjected to deduction of appropriate withholding tax and approval.

According to the notice, the dividends will be paid to shareholders whose names appear in the register of members as of the close of business on October 31, 2019.

The notice read in part, “The register of shareholders will be closed on Thursday, October 31, 2019.

“On Monday, November 11, 2019, dividends will be paid electronically to shareholders whose names appear on the register of members as of the close of business on Wednesday, October 30, 2019 and who have completed the e-dividend registration and mandated the registrar to pay their dividends directly into their bank account.

“Shareholders who are yet to complete the e-dividend registration are advised to download the registrar’s e-dividend mandate activation form, which is available on our registrars’ website.”

It added that shareholders with dividend warrants and share certificates that were unclaimed or yet to be presented for payment or returned for validation were advised to complete the e-dividend registration or contact the registrars.
