Oyo lauds Queen’s School old girls for donating classrooms
Ademola Babalola, Ibadan

The Oyo State Government on Thursday commended the old pupils of Queen’s School, Ibadan, for donating a block of classrooms to their alma mater.

Speaking during the 50th anniversary and reunion of the 1964-1968 set of the Queen’s School Old Girls Association, the Acting Permanent Secretary in the Ministry of Education, represented by a Director of Schools, Mrs. C. O. Bolaji, appreciated the efforts of the old pupils.

The acting permanent secretary commended the old girls for adding value to the quality of teaching and learning in the school.

The government also applauded the old girls for distinguishing themselves in their various fields of endeavour and rising to the pinnacle of their careers.

The coordinator of the 1964-1968 set, Taiwo Adedapo-Aisida, said the foundation of the new classroom block was laid on April 12, 2018 and its completion was made possible by her colleagues who were desirous of making the school relevant and impactful in the scheme of things.

Reacting to the donation, the Acting Principal of the school, Mrs Modupe kusaanu, noted that the gesture of the old girls was not only noteworthy, it also came handy. She gave members of the set the assurance that the pupils would make the best use of the facilities provided.

The Head girl of the school, Gold Obuseh, thanked members of the set for the donation and promised that the pupils would reciprocate their gesture in due course with better academic results.
