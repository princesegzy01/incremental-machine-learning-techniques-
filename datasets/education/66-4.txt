10 graduating students of ACE bag first class
Peter Dada, Akure

Ten graduates of Adeyemi College of Education, Ondo, Ondo State, have graduated with first class in different programmes of the institution.

The degree-awarding institution, which is affiliated to the Obafemi Awolowo University, Ile-Ife, Osun State, held its 34th convocation ceremony on its main campus, with 5,752 students bagging Bachelor degrees.

The Vice-Chancellor of the OAU, Prof. Eyitope Ogunbodede, disclosed this during the event, which was a combination of four academic sessions â€“ the 2012/2013, 2014/2015, 2015/2016 and 2016/2017 academic sessions.

According to the don, the graduates of the ACE received the same quality of education as their counterparts in the parent institution, the OAU.

Giving the breakdown, the VC said that 10 graduated with first class, 1,436 finished with Second Class Upper Division, 3,078 graduated with Second Class Lower Division, while 1,053 completed their programmes with Third Class and 175 graduated with pass.

The Provost of the college, Prof. Olukoya Ogen, in his address, said a total of 12,628 graduated at the combined convocation ceremony.

Ogen said that while 5,752 were awarded Bachelor degrees, 6,876 graduated with NCE.

He noted the institution was unable to hold convocation ceremony in the last four years due to financial constraints.
