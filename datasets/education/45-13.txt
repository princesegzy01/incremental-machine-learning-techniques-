Kola Daisi varsity slashes tuition fees
Ademola Babalola, Ibadan

The founder of Kola Daisi University and Basorun of Ibadanland, Chief Kola Daisi, has approved the reduction of the fees payable by all categories of students in the current session by at least N50,000.

The Vice-Chancellor of the institution, Prof. Kolade Ayorinde, told reporters at a press conference on Wednesday that all the students, totalling 142, were beneficiaries.

Ayorinde also hinted that the institution was the cheapest private university charging tuition which ranges from N411,000 and N491,000 and payable by the concerned students on three instalments.

Ayorinde who disclosed that no private university in the country was making money or profit, said some indigent students were currently on scholarships provided by the institution to allow them access university education.

He said, “The founder of the institution has approved the reduction of the fees by all categories of students in the current session by at least N50,000 for all 142 students of the institution.”

He said the institution currently had students from 19 out of 36 states of the federation, adding that the mission of the institution was to nurture graduates with high employability attributes and competencies to help transform Nigeria into global reckoning.
