JCI Nigeria’s Ten Outstanding Young Persons awardees emerge
Tare Youdeowei

Junior Chamber International, Nigeria, has honoured 10 youths for innovations and leadership skills in their different fields of endeavour, at the 2018 edition of the global JCI Ten Outstanding Young Persons.

This was made known by the Chairperson of the award, Ms Oluwatoyin Atanda, who said the honourees were also selected due to their rare leadership qualities. “The ten finalists that were awarded demonstrated rare leadership qualities. We can categorically say they are bound for the top considering their giant strides in the areas they chose to follow. This recognition comes as a result of their breath-taking innovations, success and leadership skills in their different fields of endeavour,” Atanda said.

On his part, President of JCI Nigeria, Mr Adeniyi Balogun, thanked the team, as well as corporate partners, for making the event a success saying that the organization would not relent in recognising the good deeds of young people. “I applaud the commitment of JCI Nigeria members and alumni, as well as local and national officers for sustaining the Junior Chamber International Ten Outstanding Young Persons programme.”

The award categories were Personal Improvement and Accomplishment, Academic Leadership and Accomplishments, Medical Innovation, Scientific and Technological Development, to mention a few.

In the past, JCIN-TOYP has honoured Dr Ola Orekunrin, Mr Ray Ekpu, Sir Shina Peters, Kanu Nwankwo, Mr Femi Anikulapo-Kuti, Daddy Showkey, Richard Mofe Damijo, Gbenga Sesan, to mention a few.
