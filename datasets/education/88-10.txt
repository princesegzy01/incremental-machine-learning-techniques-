TETFUND adjudges MAPOLY e-library best in Ogun
A resource person from the Tertiary Education Trust Fund (TETFUND), Mr Linus C. Emeghara, has adjudged Moshood Abiola Polytechnic, Abeokutaâ€™s E-Library facilities as the best among tertiary institutions in Ogun State.

He made this known during a workshop on EBSCO Host Database Training for MAPOLY staff at the E-library Centre, which is a section of the Salawu Abiola Memorial Library of the Institution.

Emeghara, who commended Management of the polytechnic, stressed that MAPOLY had more than 12 resources in e-books and journals covering all subjects and disciplines.

He urged the members of the staff of the institution to take advantage of the e-library facilities to improve on their researches, teaching and learning skills.

The Registrar, Mr. Emmanuel Adeleye, while addressing the participants disclosed that, MAPOLY had invested a lot of money to collaborate with agencies in some training and retraining programmes for members of its staff. He urged them to justify the investment by reflecting on their respective schedule of duties.

Adeleye noted that the recent focus of the management of the institution on Information Technology training for both academic and non-academic staff was aimed at repositioning the polytechnic for future challenges.

While declaring the seminar open, the Acting Rector of the polytechnic, Ayodeji Tella, appreciated TETFUND for its huge investment in educational development in Nigeria. He advised the staff of the institution to take advantage of the training to improve the system.
