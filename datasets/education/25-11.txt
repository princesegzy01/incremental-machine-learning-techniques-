FedPoly partners machine tools company on training for students
Bola Bamigbola, Osogbo

The Management of the Federal Polytechnic, Ede in Osun State has partnered the Nigeria Machine Tools in Osogbo, the state capital, on training for its students.

The partnership, which was facilitated by M8 Global Know Network, was aimed at ensuring that students of the polytechnic graduate with skills relevant to the needs of industries in the country.

Speaking after the partnership agreement was signed in Osogbo, the Head of Project and Partnership, M8 Global Know Network, Dr Loveday Egbedion, said there was a need to create a synergy between industries and academic institutions. He stressed that such a move would expose students to work practices that complement the theory taught in the classroom.

He explained that his company initiated the Federal Polytechnic Ede Assess for Collaborative Education Centre with the sole aim of assisting the school to produce graduates that were industry relevant.

The Rector of the Polytechnic, Dr John Adekolawole, in his remarks urged tertiary institutions to collaborate with industries in order to bring out the essence of polytechnic education.

Adekolawole noted that partnership with the company would assist the institution to expose the students to industrial machines in order to further equip them with knowledge that is relevant to the society.

The General Manager, Nigeria Machine Tools, Osogbo, Pratish Thakur said the firm had nurtured the plan to partner with academic institutions so as to assist them in teaching the students skills that would make them relevant to employers.
