UNIUYO lecturer emerges NES chairperson
A professor of Environmental and Industrial Microbiology, Comfort Etok, has been re-elected as the Chairperson of the Nigerian Environmental Society in Akwa Ibom State.

This was done at the Multipurpose Hall of the University of Uyo, and had the Akwa Ibom State Deputy Governor, Mr Moses Ekpo, Commissioner for Environment and Mineral Resources, Dr Iniobong Essien and the Vice Chancellor University of Uyo, Professor Enefiok Essien and other environmental stakeholders in attendance.

Etokâ€™s re-election, according to the electoral committee, was the unanimous decision of the chapter due to successes in her last tenure, among which was a quiz and essay competition on the environment for primary and secondary schools in the state, sanitary inspection of Local Government Headquarters, yearly celebration of the world Environment Day, donation of cleaning materials to faculties, tree planting, hosting environmental forums, amongst others.

Expressing gratitude for being re-elected, Etok, who was a former Head, Department of Microbiology thanked members for the confidence they had in her, as well as for their support and cooperation in her first term, as she asked for more of it in the second term.

Etok, who was also the immediate-past Chairperson of UNIUYO Table Water, further called for the establishment of an environmental research unit in the Ministries of Environment, Solid Mineral, Petroleum Resources and allied establishments in Nigeria for the investigation and mitigation of environmental issues.
