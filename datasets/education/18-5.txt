VC blames policymakers, parents for discrimination in courses
Tunji Bosun, Abeokuta

The Vice-Chancellor, Tai Solarin University of Education, Ijagun, Ogun State, Prof. Abayomi Arigbabu, has blamed policymakers, parents as well as stakeholders for the rate of discrimination in educational careers.

He said the stakeholders should be held responsible for the effects of giving preference to some courses at the expense of others.

Arigbabu stated this at this year’s edition of the Creative Arts Department Exhibition by 243 final year students of the department.

He appealed to parents to give support to their children that are artistically inclined.

While inspecting various artworks by the students, the VC said he was happy with the works exhibited by the department.

According to him, the founding fathers of the university will be happy that their experiment turned out to be a success.

The students displayed various artworks such as ceramics, sculpture, painting, textile and graphics.

Arigbabu commended lecturers of the department for their dedication and commitment to quality academic performance of the students.

While assuring the department of the management’s continued support, the Vice-Chancellor further commended the students for showcasing their works of art which he described as memorabilia.

In his submission, the Director, Centre for Vocational and Entrepreneurial Studies and also a lecturer in the department, Prof. Abiodun Banjo, described the event as a celebration of culture and the best of such exhibition in the South-Western part of Nigeria.
