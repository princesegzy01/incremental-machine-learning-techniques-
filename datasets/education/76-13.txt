Bowen University appoints Ogunwole as new vice-chancellor
Femi Makinde, Osogbo

The Governing Council of the Bowen University, Iwo, has appointed Prof. Joshua Ogunwole as the new vice-chancellor of the university established by Nigerian Baptist Convention.

Ogunwole, who is a professor of Soil Science, was appointed from the Ahmadu Bello University, Zaria in Kaduna State. He succeeded Prof. Mathews Ojo, whose five year tenure has expired.

According to a statement made available to our correspondent in Osogbo on Monday, the new vice-chancellor,who was unveiled during a service at the chapel of the university, promised to lift the standard of the private university.

Ogunwole said he was happy to have come after great administrators like Prof Okedara ; Prof Timothy Olagbemiro; and the immediate past vice chancellor, Ojo. The new VC said the the university would not relent in producing godly and competent leaders, who would transform every sector in the country and other parts of the world.

He said, “Bowen University is a Christian institution of higher learning with Excellence and Godliness as motto. It has a mission to bring forth the next generations of leaders with godliness, high performance and strong ethical character, we cannot fail God.”
