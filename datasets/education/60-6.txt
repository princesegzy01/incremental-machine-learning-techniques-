KWASU honours former Ghanaian President, two others
Tunde Oyekola, Ilorin

Kwara state University is set to honour the former President of Ghana, Mr John Kuffour, the Chief Executive Officer of Guarantee Trust Bank, Mr TajudeenAdeola, and a traditional musician, Alhaji Aminu Alao with honorary doctoral degrees.

The Vice-Chancellor of the institution, Prof. Abdulrasheed Na’allah disclosed this at a briefing held on the main campus of the university last Malete on Friday.

While Kuffour would be conferred with a doctoral degree in Public Administration (Honoris Causa), Adeola would receive a doctoral degree in Entrepreneurship and Jaigbade would be honoured with a Doctor of Literature degree.

The VC explained that Kuffour was considered for the honour because of his exemplary leadership qualities, as he allowed free election duiring his time in office.

He added that Adeola brought a lot of dynamic changes into the banking industry, which made it to be efficient describing him as a foremost entrepreneur in the country.

Na’allah disclosed that Alao would be honoured for his commitment to culture and tradition in his music composition which represented the type of tradition valued by Africans.
