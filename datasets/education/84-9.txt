60 Kwara schools for maiden Maths, English competition
Success Nwogu, Ilorin

About 60 secondary schools in Kwara State will participate in the maiden Inter-School Mathematics and English Competition scheduled to take off in the state soon. Its theme is, ‘Investment in education, the solution to all problems’.

Speaking during a briefing held in Ilorin, the state capital, on Thursday, the Coordinator of the competition, Mr. Yakubu Isowo, said the schools were drawn from the three senatorial districts of the state, namely Kwara-Central, Kwara-North and Kwara-South.

Isowo also said that 10 secondary schools with good performances at the preliminaries would be selected from each zone to participate in the grand finale of the competition, which is supported by the Kwara State Ministry of Education and Human Capital Development.

According to him, the first, second and third best schools will receive awards alongside their Mathematics teacher during the grand finale on August, 4.

The Commissioner for Education and Human Capital Development, Alhaja Bilikisu Oniyangi, said the competition would inspire and ignite pupils’ passion for Mathematics and English Language.

Oniyangi stated that EMIS was meant to provide accurate data on pupils, teachers, schools and even MDAS. She said it would make timely statistic for effective planning and policy/decision making in the educational sector to become readily available to various stakeholders on line real time.
