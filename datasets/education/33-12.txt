OOU, monarch to hold lecture on good governance
Tare Youdeowei

The Board of Trustees of the Oba Sikiru Adetona Professorial Chair in Governance and the Olabisi Onabanjo University, Ago- Iwoye, Ogun State, are set to host a third annual lecture to advocate for good governance.

The lecture, which is scheduled to hold at the Adeola Odutola Hall in Ijebu-Ode, on Friday, May 10, is themed Grassroots governance: The soft underbelly of Nigeria’s political architecture.

This was made known in a statement issued by the Awujale of Ijebuland, Oba Sikiru Adetona, who disclosed that the lecture will be delivered by Prof. Ayo Olukotun, while presidential candidates, Prof. Kingsley Moghalu and Prof. Remi Sonaiya will share their on the challenges of governance in Nigeria.

“I am committed to seeing Nigeria take giant strides in the area of good governance stemming from quality education, research, mentorship and community engagement. Education has a pivotal role to play in the quality of governance and I am using this opportunity to reiterate my desire to help the Olabisi Onabanjo University continue to produce leaders moulded for good governance,” Adetona said.

The Vice-Chancellor of the university, Prof. Ganiyu Olatunde, described the annual lecture as a legacy that would continue to drive greatness in the university and society. “Since the inception of this initiative, the university has improved on her research activities in the quest for excellence. As such, we are optimistic that we will continue to thrive in good governance, corporate and political leadership to fertilise the students and staff with a view to raising good governance champions in the Nigerian community.”
