NPFL Roundup: MFM, Plateau, Abia Warriors, Wikki win
’Tosin Oluwalowo

MFM secured the only away win as the 2019/20 Nigeria Professional Football season got underway across the nation on Sunday.

The Olukoya Boys defeated Heartland 1-0 at the Dan Anyiam Stadium, Owerri through a 40th minute goal by Clement Ogwu.

Abia Warriors defeated Katsina United 2-0 through a Oni Jimoh double, while Lucio Samuel’s goal ensured Ifeanyi Ubah defeated Adamaway United 1-0.

Plateau United were 3-1 winners over Lobi Stars at the New Jos stadium, Jos. Silas Nenrot and a Solomon Kwambe’s own goal put Plateau 2-0 up, before Alimi Sikiru’s penalty reduced the deficit. Amos Gyang was however on hand to make it 3-1, scoring in the 67th minute.

Wikki Tourist scored two goals in the second half through Saidu Abubakar in the 47th minute and Idris Guda in the 61st minute to cancel out Lala Abdullahi’s 15th minute strike, running away with a 2-1 win at the Abubakar Tafawa Balewa Stadium Bauchi.

There were also three draws recorded on matchday one of the NPFL

Bala Yahuza’s 79th minute goal salvaged a point for Delta Force at home to Akwa Starlets, after Isaac George had shot the visiting side into the lead in the 50th minute.

Akwa United also played 1-1 draw against Warri Wolves at the Warri Township stadium. Amadi Samuel put Wolves ahead in the second minute, before Mfon Udoh equalised for the Uyo-based side in the 31st minute.

At the Sani Abacha stadium, Kano Pillars played a goalless draw with Rivers United

Enyimba versus Nasarawa United and Rangers versus Sunshine Stars were postponed due to their involvement in the CAF Confederations Cup.

Week two matches will be played by 4:00pm on November 10.
