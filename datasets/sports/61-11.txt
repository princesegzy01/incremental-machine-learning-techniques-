Downpour disrupts Lagos Open finals
’Tosin oluwalowo

The men and women’s singles finals of the second leg of the Lagos Open could not hold on Sunday as heavy rains disrupted proceedings.

The rains, which started early on Sunday, fell throughout the day, making it impossible for the game to go on.

The final, which was meant to bring to an end the two-week event, has winner of the men’s singles in the first leg, Calvin Hemery, up against fifth seed Aldin Setkic.

In the women’s singles final, India’s, Riya Bhatia is up against Nasja Kolar.

Hemery qualified for the final after beating Tunisian Aziz Ouakaa 7-6 6-2 to win his ninth consecutive match in the semi-finals in Lagos.

Setkic, a winner here in 2015, out-classed sixth seed, Takanyi Garanganga, 6-2, 6-2 in 73 minutes.

Bhatia beat compatriot Rutuja Bhosale 4-6, 7-6, 7-5 in the semi-finals, while Kolar, who’s aiming for her second ITF title this year, defeated second seed, Valeriya Strakhova, 6-3, 3-6, 6-1.
