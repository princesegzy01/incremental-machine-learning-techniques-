AFCON 2019: Aina made us proud, says family
’Tosin Oluwalowo

Torino defender Ola Aina, said the opportunity to represent the Super Eagles at the Africa Cup of Nations in Egypt this year completed a circle in his life.

Speaking with Al Jazeera, Aina who had previously played for England at the youth level said going on to represent Nigeria at a major tournament was a dream come true.

Aina who played in five games at the AFCON, made the assist for Nigeria’s first goal at the tournament, scored by Odion Ighalo in the 1-0 win over Burundi.

He said, “It was completing a circle in my life having played for England at youth level. To then play for Nigeria at a major tournament – the buzz after that assist was amazing.

“To play five matches in seven at that tournament and to win a third-place medal is the stuff of dreams.”

His uncle, Abayomi Aina, also said the defender’s exploits at the AFCON was celebrated back home in Nigeria.

“It was all over the news, (Ola) gave our folks something to be proud of back home,” said his uncle

“I was overjoyed and the fact that it’s my nephew, the name Aina being mentioned by commentators.

“In a country of about 200 million people, his name was on the lips of millions from Lagos to Ijebu Ikenne (the family’s ancestral hometown) and it was indeed a proud moment for us.”

Aina moved permanently to Torino in the summer after spending last season on loan there from Chelsea.

He has featured in 10 games for the Italian side this season.
