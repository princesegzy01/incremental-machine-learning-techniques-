87 chairmanship, 670 councillorship candidates for Niger election
Enyioha Opara

Ahead of this month’s local government elections in Niger State, the State Independent Electoral Commission, on Monday, cleared 87 chairmanship and 670 councillorship candidates from nine political parties.

The All Progressives Congress and the Peoples Democratic Party have an equal number of 25 chairmanship and 274 councillorship contestants.

The All Progressive Grand Alliance is fielding nine chairmanship and 48 councillorship candidates followed by the GPN which has eight chairmanship and seven councillorship contestants and the ANP with seven chairmanship and nine councillorship contestants.

The NNPP is fielding four chairmanship and nine councillorship candidates, while the SDP and the NCP are fielding one each of chairmanship and councillorship candidates.

The Chairman of the commission, Aminu Alhaji, who spoke at the opening of a three-day ‘Train the Trainers Workshop,’ said everything was set for the election taking place on November 30.

The event was organised in partnership with the International Foundation for Electoral System for electoral officers and assistant electoral officers in Minna.

The SIEC chairman said, “This training is to prepare you for and acquaint you with all that you need to conduct a free and transparent election.”
