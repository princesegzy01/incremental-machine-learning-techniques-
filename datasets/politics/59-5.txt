Banditry: Masari holds meeting with herdsmen, security chiefs
Olaide Oyelude, Katsina

Governor Aminu Masari of Katsina State on Thursday held a special meeting with representatives of herdsmen, heads of security agencies and transition committee chairmen in eight local government areas in the state that share borders with Zamfara State.

The meeting was to find final solutions to the renewed bandit attacks and kidnappings in the state.

Also in attendance at the meeting which took place at the state secretariat complex in Katsina were Deputy Governor, Alhaji Manir Yakubu; and Secretary to the State Government, Alhaji Inuwa Muhammed, as well as leaders of Miyyetti Allah in the state.

Sources at the meeting disclosed that Governor Masari told who were in attendance that he was ready to go and meet the bandits at any venue of their choice to resolve the current security menace in the state.

A source said, “The governor who spoke in Hausa said he was ready to find a lasting solution to the problem.”
