Yobe gov presents N108.4bn budget
Kayode Idowu, Damaturu

The Yobe State Governor, Alhaji Mai-Mala Bunu, on Wednesday, presented an appropriation bill of N108.4bn to the state House of Assembly.

The budget showed an increase of N16.7bn or 15.5 per cent over the budget of N92.2bn for the current fiscal year.

The recurrent expenditure is to get N58bn or 53.8 per cent, while capital expenditure stands to get N50.4bn or 46.5 per cent.

The breakdown showed that education got the largest share, followed by health and then works, with N22.9bn, N16.1bn and N12.7bn.

The governor, who tagged the bill, ‘Budget of Continuity and Consolidation,’ said, “It is about the lives of our people, values and about our vision, mission, commitment to work hard, sacrifice and resolute determination to move our state forward to the path of socioeconomic prosperity.”

He said it would build on “the momentum” to revitalise the education sector and seek to boost agriculture by encouraging a transition from traditional to commercial farming.

Bunu also promised improved access to qualitative and affordable health care by upgrading health care infrastructure and making sure that hospitals, clinics, and health centres had the basic requirements to function efficiently and effectively.

He stated, “We will also build and upgrade critical infrastructure such as roads, water supply and sanitation facilities and electricity, among others, to accelerate our effort in post-insurgency resettlement, rehabilitation, reconstruction and peace-building.”
