‘I won’t interfere in Ibadan chieftaincy system’
Ademola Babalola, Ibadan

The Oyo State Governor, Mr Seyi Makinde, has assured the Olubadan of Ibadan, Oba Saliu Adetunji, and other Ibadan indigenes that he will not interfere in the state traditional council system.

Makinde spoke on Tuesday during a areception to mark the installation of the 14th Iyalode of Ibadanland, Chief Theresa Oyekanmi, and the celebration of her 86th birthday at the International Conference Centre, University of Ibadan.

Makinde, who was represented by the Commissioner for Local Government and Chieftaincy Matters, Mrs Funmi Orisadeyi, also solicited the support of the traditional council for the progress of the state.

He said “I pray that God grant Iyalode long life to serve the people. I want to reiterate my commitment not to interfere in the traditional chieftaincy system in Ibadan.

“My resolve is that as your son, I am determined to support you and bring glory to our traditional system and institution.

“I also seek your cooperation in this regard. God bless our town, state and country.”

Earlier, the Olubadan prayed for Oyekanmi for long life and fortune.
