World Bank, governors’ forum laud Ekiti on development
Abiodun Nejo, Ado Ekiti

The World Bank has congratulated the Ekiti State Government for emerging the best performing state under the National Urban Water Sector Reform Project and third Best Performing State under the Community and Social Development Project.

The Chief Press Secretary to the governor, Yinka Oyebode, in a statement, said the recognition was part of Nigeria Portfolio Performance Awards for 2019 communicated through a letter signed by World Bank’s Country Director for Nigeria and Africa Region, Rachid Banmessaoud.

According to Oyebode, the award recognises the performance of project implementation teams across the states and federal institutions.

He stated that Banmesaoud, in the letter addressed to Governor Kayode Fayemi, said, “This is to congratulate you on your state’s commendable performance which led to winning the award for the Best Performing State under the NUWSRP 3 and third Best Performing State under the CSDP.

“We hope to leverage on the gains and outcomes of this event as we look forward to a deepened partnership in your state.”

Oyebode stated that the Progressives Governors Forum had also praised the state government for recording the highest number of initiatives in the Forum’s Development Initiatives tracking for the month of August.

According to him, the PGF, in a letter signed by Mr Foluso Aluko, for the Director General, said the Ekiti State led with 18 initiatives ahead of Nasarawa and Lagos which recorded 14 and 10 respectively.

The initiatives, according to the letter, spread across empowerment, job creation, health care, education, infrastructure, security, economy, agriculture and justice.
