Ugochinyere resigns as IPAC spokesperson
Success Nwogu, Abuja

The Chairman of the Action Peoples Party, Mr. Imo Ugochinyere, has resigned as the National Publicity Secretary of the Inter-Party Advisory Council.

Ugochinyere, in his letter to the National Chairman of IPAC, Mr Peter Ameh, and made available to our correspondent in Abuja on Wednesday, said his resignation was with immediate effect.

He stated in the letter, a copy of which was sent to the Chairman, Independent National Electoral Commission, Prof. Mahmood Yakubu, that he would not seek re-election during the upcoming IPAC National Executive Committee election.

The letter read in part, “After consultation with my associates and family, I have decided to resign my position as IPAC National Publicity Secretary with immediate effect and also announce my intention not to seek re-election at the upcoming IPAC National Executive Committee election.

“My resignation was based on the need to allow a neutral person to speak for IPAC.”
