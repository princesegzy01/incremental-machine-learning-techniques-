APC NWC upholds panels reports on Bayelsa
Kamarudeen Ogundele, Abuja

The National Working Committee of the All Progressives Congress has upheld the reports of the screening and appeal committees for the Bayelsa State governorship aspirants.

Of the seven governorship aspirants that were screened, six were cleared to participate in the governorship primary.

They are Mr Aganaba Steven, Senator Heineken Lokpobiri, Prince Ebitimi Amgbare, Ms Diseye Poweigha, Lyon Pereworimini, and Prof Ongoebi Etebu.

A statement by the APC National Publicity Secretary, Lanre Issa-onilu, on Tuesday said a former Resident Electoral Commissioner in Cross River State, Dr Briyai Frankland, was not cleared to participate in the primary.

The statement said, “The APC NWC upheld the disqualification of Dr Briyai Oyeins Frankland based on the party’s guidelines for the nomination of candidates which requires that aspirants are card-carrying members of the party for one year.

“There was record of his membership of the APC. He was considered ineligible.”
