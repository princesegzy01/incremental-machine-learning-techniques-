100 days: Makinde inaugurates six model schools
Ademola Babalola, Ibadan

The Governor of Oyo State, Mr Seyi Makinde, will inaugurate six model schools, constructed under the Universal Basic Education Commission/Oyo State Universal Basic Education Board intervention projects across the three senatorial districts of the state.

The inauguration is part of activities marking his 100 days in office.

Also, government has promised speedy completion of all ongoing UBEC/SUBEB projects in the state, before the end of the year.

It assured the people of the state that pupils in all public schools would enjoy a better lease of life in their basic educational pursuits.

The Chairman, Oyo State Universal Basic Education Board, Dr Nureni Adeniran, said it was part of efforts by the government to provide quality education to indigent pupils in the state.

He revealed that six model schools across the state were ready for inauguration.

He said, “This attests to the kind and genuine gestures the governor has in rejigging the education sector of this state.

“The model school projects are located in Ibadan, Oyo and Oke- Ogun area of the state.”

Adeniran stated that the governor’s pro-activeness and commitment to the development of education in the state, enhanced the speedy completion of the projects.

He said the current administration would ensure that education was not only made free for pupils across the state, but that the government would make the schools pupil-friendly.
