Police tracking UI hostel invaders, says VC
Ademola Babalola, Ibadan

The Vice Chancellor of the University of Ibadan, Prof Idowu Olayinka, has said the police are currently tracking invaders of one of the hostels in the institution, Abdulsalam Abubakar Hall.

The Chairman of the Council Committee on Security, Prof Ademola Aremu, also stated that the university was reviewing the hostel’s security design.

The postgraduate hostel of the university was attacked on Sunday by armed men.

About seven men who were armed with machetes entered the female block of the AbdusalamiAbubakar Hall through a window and raided the first and second floors.

It was gathered that the security man on patrol was tied up by the armed men who attacked two students with machetes.

But Aremu, who visited the hall in company with the vice chancellor, stated that the police were working to track the invaders.

The vice chancellor also said the institution “is on top of the situation.”

Olayinka said, “The invaders have shown pattern in their operation by attacking female students during holidays when halls are sparsely populated.”

The police Public Relations Officer, Mr Olugbenga Fadeyi told said, “We are reviewing our security architecture. Soon, there will be a clampdown on the fleeing invaders.”
